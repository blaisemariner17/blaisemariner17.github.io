About

Bioinformatics researcher focused on aging and age-associated deterioration. I build robust, scalable analysis pipelines (R, Python, Nextflow, SLURM) and translate complex genomic results into clear, actionable insights. I have extensive experience working with data across various organisms — from simple to complex — including, but not limited to, yeast, nematodes, mice, dogs, and humans.

  • Expertise: DNA methylation, quantitative trait loci (QTL), genome-wide association studies (GWAS), next-generation sequencing (NGS) analysis (e.g. RNA-seq)
  • Tools: R (tidyverse, Bioconductor), Python, PLINK/GCTA, Nextflow/Snakemake; version control with GitHub
  • Compute: HPC at scale, cloud-ready workflows, containerized and reproducible environments

Selected Scientific Contributions

  • Autophagy activation by reverse transcriptase inhibitors (Ph.D.): Demonstrated that reverse transcriptase inhibitors can activate autophagy in mammals via conserved hormetic pathways, supported by many wet lab assays and RNA sequencing.
  • Aging and transposable elements (Postdoc): Identified accelerated methylation erosion at transposable elements in larger dog breeds using a 900+ sample Dog Aging Project dataset.
  • Cross-institutional bioinformatics: I continue to deliver multi-omics analyses, statistical modeling, and reproducible pipelines across collaborations with multiple academic groups.

Scientific Values

  • Integrity first: Data-driven hypotheses, transparent reporting, and careful interpretation.
  • Reproducibility by default: Containerized workflows, code review, and open, version-controlled analyses.
  • Clarity and impact: Communicating results plainly to enable decisions in research and product contexts.
